About ALIU Interface: ALIU Red Flag Artwork Database

Introduction

ALIU Interface is a specialized database platform focusing on artworks previously identified with an ALIU Red Flag status. This designation indicates potential involvement in looting or illegal transactions during World War II. Our platform serves as a resource for researchers, historians, and art professionals, providing detailed information on these specific art objects.

Objective

Our primary objective is to offer a comprehensive and factual account of artworks with a history of ALIU Red Flag status. The platform aims to support research in art history, provenance studies, and restitution efforts by providing accurate and accessible data.

Features

  • ALIU Red Flag Artwork Records: A curated database of art objects flagged by the ALIU, complete with images, descriptions, and historical data.
  • Ownership History Tracking: Detailed records of the ownership history of each artwork, emphasizing periods during and after World War II.
  • Search Functionality: Advanced search tools enable users to filter artworks based on criteria relevant to their ALIU Red Flag status and historical context.
  • Informative Context: Each entry is supplemented with historical information to provide a comprehensive understanding of the artwork's background.

Functionality

ALIU Interface utilizes SPARQL queries to extract data from Wikidata, focusing on artworks associated with ALIU Red Flag status. The platform is designed for ease of use, catering to both academic research and professional inquiry.

Audience

The database is intended for use by academic researchers, art historians, museum professionals, and others engaged in the study or management of art with complex historical backgrounds.

Collaboration and Feedback

We encourage users to contribute to the database's accuracy and comprehensiveness. Feedback and suggestions for improvement are welcomed and can be submitted through our contact page.
